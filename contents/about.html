About Me

Hi, I’m Kashif Shaikh, a final-year Game Design student who loves the way games can entertain, tell stories, release frustration, and just make you feel better after a long day.

I’d call myself a bit lazy, but I always get things done on time — just in smarter ways. I enjoy exploring new topics instead of sticking to one thing forever, which has helped me learn a lot along the way.

Pretty comfortable with Unity, I can write most of the code I need with a little AI help. Over the past year, I’ve also worked with Unreal Engine and gained solid experience in 3D modeling and texturing using Maya and Blender. I’ve experimented with AR and VR too, focusing mainly on interesting ideas and interactions.

What excites me most is working on concepts that feel interesting, even if I know I can’t finish them alone — like building an AI assistant similar to Jarvis or designing a game that teaches financial independence.

Creativity keeps me going, whether it’s digital or physical. My goal isn’t to make the most realistic or graphically advanced games — I want them to be fun, responsive, and deeply satisfying, where players truly feel in control.

My mind is always running with thoughts, day and night, and I can’t wait to bring some of them to life.
